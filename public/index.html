Daily Schedule

All times Eastern
3:00 AM
Settle + End-of-Day Report

Settles all of yesterday's bets (west coast games are done by now). Generates a P&L report and sends it to Discord. Then checks if any game today starts before 10am ET — if so, runs the full morning pipeline immediately so bets are ready before first pitch.

7:00 AM
Early Slate Fetch

Pulls today's game schedule and refreshes pitcher statcast data. Gives us a heads-up on who's pitching before Kalshi opens for the day.

8:30 AM
Full Morning Pipeline

Runs the complete pre-game sequence: fetch schedule → fetch pitcher stats → calculate edges for every strikeout market → build today's bet schedule → write portfolio plan. Skipped if the 3am early-game check already ran it.

Every 5 min
(4am – 8pm)
Lineup Check + Bet Firing

Checks whether each team's official batting lineup has been posted. The moment a lineup is confirmed, pre-built bets for that pitcher fire within 5 minutes. If lineups still aren't posted 90 minutes before first pitch, bets fire as a backstop regardless.

Every 30 min
(8:30am – 3:30pm)
Schedule & Odds Refresh

Re-fetches the game schedule and rebuilds the bet schedule. Catches any roster scratches, postponements, or lineup changes that happen after the morning run.

3:30 PM
Lineup Refresh + Re-Price

Fetches the official 9-man lineups (typically posted by now for evening games) and re-runs edge calculations with final lineup data. Updates the portfolio plan with fresh sizing before late-afternoon games.

4, 6, 8, 10 PM
Mid-Game Partial Settles

Resolves guaranteed YES wins as they happen — if a pitcher has already hit a strikeout threshold, we settle it rather than waiting until 3am.

Every Monday
8:00 AM
Model Calibration Check

Compares predicted vs. actual strikeout rates over the last 90 days. Alerts if model accuracy has drifted more than 7% — a sign the underlying pitcher data may be stale or the model needs retraining.

Decision Rules

Why we place — or skip — each bet
Rule A
Block expensive NO when model agrees with market

If the market is pricing a pitcher's K threshold as likely (NO at 65¢ or higher) AND our model also thinks he'll get there (≥50% probability), we skip the NO bet. Paying 65¢ for a NO when we think YES is likely is a losing bet structurally.

Rule D
Block YES bets without enough conviction

YES bets require our model to give at least a 30% probability. Below that, we don't have enough conviction — backing a long shot at low confidence burns capital even when the price looks right.

Rule E
Block NO when market has already priced it in

If the market is pricing NO below 15¢ — meaning the market is nearly certain the pitcher won't reach the threshold — there's almost no edge left to capture. We skip rather than chase pennies on a near-certain outcome.

Rule F
Block NO on low strikeout thresholds (≤4 Ks)

Historical data shows NO bets on thresholds of 4 or fewer strikeouts have poor win rates. Even when the model sees edge here, live results have been consistently negative — so we skip them entirely.

Rule G
Cap sizing when we're wildly more optimistic than the market

If our model is more than 4× more confident in a YES than the market (e.g. we say 40% chance, market says 9%), we cap the edge used for sizing at 15¢. A huge disagreement usually means model error, not a free lunch — so we size conservatively.

Lineup
Gate
No bets until the batting lineup is confirmed

We never place a bet until the official starting lineup is posted for that team. A scratch or DH replacement can completely change the strikeout environment for a pitcher. The T-90 backstop (90 min before first pitch) fires if lineups are unusually late.

YES Cap
Max 3 YES bets per pitcher per day

We limit YES exposure on any single pitcher to 3 contracts. If a pitcher has multiple K thresholds where we see edge (e.g. 5+, 6+, 7+), we take the best 3 rather than pyramiding the same risk across all of them.
