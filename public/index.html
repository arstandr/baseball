Free Money Rules

When the market hasn't caught up to reality — taker orders, no waiting

Normal bets use maker orders — we set a price and wait for someone to take the other side. Free money situations are different: the outcome is already structurally decided (or nearly so), but Kalshi's market hasn't repriced yet. In these cases, we skip the queue and use taker orders — buying immediately at whatever price is available. Speed matters more than fees here.

Pulled
Pitcher
Pitcher removed from game — buy NO immediately

When a starter is pulled mid-game and he hasn't reached a strikeout threshold yet, that threshold is now impossible to hit. The YES contract is worthless. But Kalshi sometimes takes a few minutes to reprice. When we confirm the starter is out (reliever on mound, or same IP/Ks two polls in a row), we fire taker NO orders right away — buying the guaranteed outcome before the market catches up. Capped at $10 per threshold, $30 total per pitcher.

Exception: If we already hold a pre-game YES bet on a given threshold, we skip the free-money NO on that same threshold. Holding both sides of the same Kalshi market creates a "mixed position" that causes the settlement API to return zero revenue — breaking P&L accounting. The pre-game YES is already a losing position in this scenario; we accept that loss rather than creating an accounting mess by layering a NO on top.

Crossed
YES
Threshold already hit — buy YES if market is lagged

The opposite of a pulled pitcher: if a starter already has enough strikeouts to cover a threshold (e.g. he has 8 Ks and the market is 8+), that YES contract is worth 100¢ — guaranteed. If Kalshi is still showing YES available at ≤20¢, we buy it immediately. The outcome is certain; we're just collecting what the market hasn't priced in yet. Capped at $10 per threshold, same $30 pitcher cap as above.

Blowout
NO
Team losing big in late innings — pitcher pull is likely, buy NO

When a pitcher's team is losing by 5 or more runs starting in the 6th inning (or later), managers almost always pull their starter early. There's no reason to keep the ace in when the game is out of reach. If the pitcher still needs 3+ Ks to cover a threshold, that gap is structurally very hard to close before the hook. We buy NO taker orders in this situation — not as a certainty, but as a high-probability structural bet that the market usually underprices. Edge threshold: 10¢ or better after fees.

Dead
Path
High pitch count + too big a gap — physically can't get there, buy NO

Even if the pitcher is still in the game, some situations are structurally dead: if he's thrown 85+ pitches in 4+ innings and needs 3+ more strikeouts to cover a threshold, there just isn't enough gas left to do it. Starters rarely survive past 100–105 pitches, and each inning is only 3 outs — strikeouts aren't guaranteed. When pitch count and the K gap both say "not happening," we use a Kelly-sized NO taker rather than waiting. Capped at $10 per threshold via game reserves.

Pre-Game
Scratch
Scheduled starter never showed up — hedge open YES positions

Sometimes a pitcher is scratched at the last minute (injury, illness, manager decision) and a different starter takes the ball. If we placed YES bets on the scheduled starter and he never appears — confirmed by IP=0, Ks=0, not current pitcher, reliever on mound after inning 3 — we fire NO takers on any open YES positions to hedge the loss. Two-poll confirmation prevents false triggers from MLB API delays. The system treats this like a pulled pitcher: outcome determined, buy NO immediately.

Daily Schedule

All times Eastern
3:00 AM
Settle + End-of-Day Report

Settles all of yesterday's bets (west coast games are done by now). Generates a P&L report and sends it to Discord. Then checks if any game today starts before 10am ET — if so, runs the full morning pipeline immediately so bets are ready before first pitch.

7:00 AM
Early Slate Fetch

Pulls today's game schedule and refreshes pitcher statcast data. Gives us a heads-up on who's pitching before Kalshi opens for the day.

8:30 AM
Full Morning Pipeline

Runs the complete pre-game sequence: fetch schedule → fetch pitcher stats → calculate edges for every strikeout market → build today's bet schedule → write portfolio plan. Skipped if the 3am early-game check already ran it.

Every 5 min
(4am – 8pm)
Lineup Check + Bet Firing

Checks whether each team's official batting lineup has been posted. The moment a lineup is confirmed, pre-built bets for that pitcher fire within 5 minutes. If lineups still aren't posted 90 minutes before first pitch, bets fire as a backstop regardless.

Every 30 min
(8:30am – 3:30pm)
Schedule & Odds Refresh

Re-fetches the game schedule and rebuilds the bet schedule. Catches any roster scratches, postponements, or lineup changes that happen after the morning run.

2:00 PM
Afternoon Pitcher Data Refresh

Re-fetches Statcast K% data from Baseball Savant. The 7am fetch can be 7+ hours stale by game time — this ensures evening-game edge calculations use the freshest available pitcher data before bets fire.

3:30 PM
Lineup Refresh + Re-Price

Fetches the official 9-man lineups (typically posted by now for evening games) and re-runs edge calculations with final lineup data. Updates the portfolio plan with fresh sizing before late-afternoon games.

4, 6, 8, 10 PM
Mid-Game Partial Settles

Resolves guaranteed YES wins as they happen — if a pitcher has already hit a strikeout threshold, we settle it rather than waiting until 3am.

Every Monday
8:00 AM
Model Calibration Check

Compares predicted vs. actual strikeout rates over the last 90 days. Alerts if model accuracy has drifted more than 7% — a sign the underlying pitcher data may be stale or the model needs retraining.

Decision Rules

Why we place — or skip — each bet
Rule A
Block expensive NO when model agrees with market

If the market is pricing a pitcher's K threshold as likely (NO at 65¢ or higher) AND our model also thinks he'll get there (≥50% probability), we skip the NO bet. Paying 65¢ for a NO when we think YES is likely is a losing bet structurally.

Rule D
Block low-conviction YES bets — unless the edge is massive

YES bets normally require at least 25% model probability. Below that threshold, we skip — backing a long shot at low confidence wastes capital. Exception: if the edge is 18¢ or greater (market is significantly mispriced), the bet is allowed through regardless of model conviction, since large edges override low-confidence calls.

Rule E
Block NO when market has already priced it in

If the market is pricing NO below 15¢ — meaning the market is nearly certain the pitcher won't reach the threshold — there's almost no edge left to capture. We skip rather than chase pennies on a near-certain outcome.

Rule F
Block NO on low strikeout thresholds (≤4 Ks)

Historical data shows NO bets on thresholds of 4 or fewer strikeouts have poor win rates. Even when the model sees edge here, live results have been consistently negative — so we skip them entirely.

Lineup
Gate
No bets until BOTH teams' lineups are confirmed

We wait for the official batting lineup from both the home and away team before firing any bet. A single team's lineup isn't enough — the opposing lineup determines how many strikeout opportunities the pitcher actually faces. The T-90 backstop (90 min before first pitch) fires if lineups are still missing.

YES Cap
Max 3 YES bets per pitcher per day

We limit YES exposure on any single pitcher to 3 contracts. If a pitcher has multiple K thresholds where we see edge (e.g. 5+, 6+, 7+), we take the best 3 rather than pyramiding the same risk across all of them.

TTO
Penalty
15% K-rate reduction the 3rd time through the lineup

When a pitcher faces the same batters for the 3rd time in a game (roughly inning 7+), hitters have seen all his pitches and adjust — K rates historically drop ~15%. Both the pre-game model and the live in-game model apply the same 15% reduction, so our probability estimates stay consistent throughout the game.
